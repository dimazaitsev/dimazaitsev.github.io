References for Clan Composition to read, watch, run, cite, and join

Two matrix rows are near if they contain a nonzero value of the same sign with the same index; transitive closure of nearness relation represents a clan. The clan composition speeds-up solving sparse systems. I wonder whether it is applicable in social sciences.

First introduced for bpartite directed graphs (of Petri nets) as functional subnets or subnets with input and output places they were generalized on arbitrary matrixes over a rigng with a sign and called clans.

Thirteen yeas after the Cover Picture Story of Petri Net Newsletter have passed until Jack Dongarra recognized clans' usefullness to speed-up solving linear systems on parallel and distributed architecture and invited me for a Fulbright project, a joint paper published, a joint software ParAd issued.
